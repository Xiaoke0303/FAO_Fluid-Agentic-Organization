## 1. Why This Note Exists / 本笔记为何存在

Recent FAO discussions reveal an offset risk: the framework over-focuses on AI Agent capabilities, permissions, execution boundaries, pseudo-completion, and responsibility drift, while under-addressing the human side of new organizational forms.

This note records a new judgment:

> **In agentic organizations, humans are not merely supervisors of agents; they are also governed subjects whose attention, judgment, and responsibility-bearing capacity must be protected.**

中文：

> **在智能体组织中，人不只是智能体的监督者；人本身也是被治理的主体，其注意力、判断力和责任承载能力都需要被保护。**

## 2. Human Finitude as Organizational Condition / 人类有限性作为组织条件

Humans are not infinitely rational, infinitely alert, infinitely attentive, or infinitely responsible. These are not weaknesses to eliminate but conditions to design around:

- **finite life** / 生命有限
- **fear** / 恐惧
- **limited attention** / 注意力有限
- **cognitive fatigue** / 认知疲劳
- **emotional fluctuation** / 情绪波动
- **responsibility pressure** / 责任压力
- **need for meaning** / 意义需求
- **social and political embeddedness** / 被关系和组织政治嵌入

These traits are neutral. They become problems only when organizational design ignores them.

---

## 3. From Agent Governance to Human-Agent Organization Governance

**Old bias**:
- Agent may overstep → govern Agent
- Agent may pseudo-complete → require Truth-State
- Agent may drift responsibility → require boundaries and audit

**New supplement**:
- Humans may also state-drift
- Humans may be pulled by dashboards, KPIs, audits, customers, regulators, superiors, and Agent outputs
- Humans may push systems toward risk or excessive caution due to fear, FOMO, responsibility pressure, or time pressure
- Humans also need structural protection, not default assumption as perfect supervisors

**Compressed judgment**:

> **FAO should not remain only an Agent Governance framework; it must evolve toward Human-Agent Organization Governance.**

中文：

> **FAO 不能停留在智能体治理框架，而应走向人—智能体组织治理框架。**

## 4. Attention and Regulation / 注意力与规制

In AI-enabled organizations, human attention is contested by:

- Agent outputs / 智能体输出
- Dashboards and metrics / 看板与指标
- Audit and compliance / 审计与合规
- Managerial targets / 管理目标
- Customer pressure / 客户压力
- Regulator expectations / 监管期待
- Peer comparison / 同侪比较
- Self-proof pressure / 自我证明压力
- FOMO / fear of missing out / 错失恐惧

Organizational theory has always regulated humans. Traditional mechanisms are not merely process controls; they govern attention, judgment drift, and responsibility pressure.

## 5. Traditional Organizational Mechanisms as Human-State Governance

| Traditional Mechanism | Human-State Governance Function |
|-----------------------|--------------------------------|
| Maker-checker separation / 经办审核分离 | Prevents single-point judgment drift |
| Review / 复核 | Brakes for fatigue, omission, overconfidence |
| Authorization hierarchy / 授权分级 | Limits individual action radius |
| Documentation / 留痕 | Enables retrospective accountability |
| Escalation / 升级 | Acknowledges frontline judgment boundaries |
| Rotation / 轮岗 | Reduces path dependency and relationship固化 |
| Segregation of duties / 岗位分离 | Prevents conflict of interest and single-point overload |

> **Traditional organizations have always regulated humans, not only tasks.**

中文：

> **传统组织一直在规制人，而不只是规制任务。**

## 6. Guarantee-Line Implication / 对保函线的启示

Maker-checker separation in bank guarantee operations is not merely a legacy workflow; it is a classic mechanism for governing human attention, judgment drift, and responsibility pressure.

It acknowledges:

- Makers fatigue
- Makers omit
- Makers are pulled by customers, front desks, and time pressure
- Checkers may also be overly conservative or path-dependent
- Complex terms, long-cycle projects, and underlying transaction disputes amplify judgment drift
- Therefore guarantee back-ends need both flow and pause

> **Maker-checker separation in bank guarantee operations is not merely a legacy workflow; it is a classic mechanism for governing human attention, judgment drift, and responsibility pressure.**

中文：

> **保函业务中的经办审核分离不是旧流程包袱，而是治理人类注意力、判断漂移和责任压力的经典机制。**

> **AI should not simply replace the maker or the checker; it should force a redesign of flow, pause, review, escalation, and responsibility anchoring.**

中文：

> **AI 不应只是简单替代经办或审核，而应推动重新设计流动、停顿、复核、升级与责任锚定。**
